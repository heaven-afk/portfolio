Community Service & Retention

Data-Driven Growth Strategies and Engagement Optimization

Overview

This case study demonstrates community analytics, engagement optimization, and retention strategies across multiple gaming communities. Work includes data-informed decision making, growth tracking, and scalable community operations.

Major Gaming League

Analytics & Insights

Role: Discord Server Builder & Administrator | Timeframe: Sep 2025 – Jan 2026

Context & Approach

Leveraged Discord's native analytics to track community health, identify growth patterns, and inform operational decisions for a 3,000+ member esports community. Focus on converting acquisition data into actionable retention strategies.

Server Growth

Membership Trajectory

Net Change: +616 (+25.9%)
Peak (Jan 2, 2026): 3,008
Interpretation: Consistent 25% growth over 4 months confirms positive operational health. Spike periods indicate successful promotional events or content releases.
Acquisition

Source Attribution

Vanity URL (Nov 14): ~96.3%
Vanity URL (Dec 14): ~94.8%
Vanity URL (Jan 13): ~89.0%
Interpretation: Strong brand recognition with 80-96% of joins via custom vanity URL. Indicates marketing effectiveness but highlights concentration risk if the vanity URL is compromised.
Engagement

Activation & Signals

New Members Communicating: 2-8%
Peak Messages (Nov 14): 10,536
Peak Voice Minutes (Nov 14): 1,523
Interpretation: High channel-opening rate (40%) vs. low first-message rate (8%) suggests onboarding friction. Members explore but hesitate to engage initially—supports automation improvements.
Retention

Churn Analysis

Early Churn (<1 month) Oct: ~11.6%
Early Churn (<1 month) Nov: ~40.0%
Early Churn (<1 month) Dec: ~31.4%
Interpretation: First 30 days are critical. High early churn (11-40% depending on cohort) confirms that onboarding and initial experience are key operational levers.

Onward Gaming Republic

Engagement & Operations

Role: Community Manager | 5,000+ members

Visit OGR Server

Context & Challenges

Served as Community Manager for Onward Gaming Republic, responsible for overall community health, engagement strategies, and member retention. The community required operational improvements in participation, retention, dispute management, and scalable processes.

Actions Taken

  • Moderation Framework: Implemented consistent moderation policies and trained the mod team
  • Community Initiatives: Organized tournaments, events, and content creation opportunities
  • Retention Strategies: Developed member engagement programs and recognition systems
  • Dispute Resolution: Established fair and transparent processes for handling conflicts
  • Stakeholder Communication: Regular reporting to leadership on community health metrics

Impact & Metrics

Outcome

Achieved healthier community operations with sustained growth. Members reported higher satisfaction with event programming and dispute handling. The moderation team operated more efficiently with clear guidelines, and leadership had better visibility into community trends through regular reporting.

Nova Gaming Network

Foundation & Growth

Role: Founder | Timeframe: June 2024 – Present

Context & Approach

Built Nova Gaming Network from scratch as a Tier 1 CODM BR scrims community, prioritizing sustainable growth over viral spikes. The approach focused on quality engagement, strong retention, and brand-driven acquisition to create a stable foundation for competitive operations.

Server Growth

Steady Foundation Building

Start (Nov 27, 2025): 1,208
Current (Jan 21, 2026): 1,238
Net Growth: +30 (+2.5%)
Time Period: 56 days
Strategy: Controlled growth prioritizing member quality over quantity. Daily fluctuations rarely exceed ±3 members, indicating exceptionally low churn and high community satisfaction.
Engagement Quality

Above-Industry Communication Rate

October (459 visitors): 15.0%
November (467 visitors): 17.3%
December (423 visitors): 18.4%
January (328 visitors): 14.0%
Benchmark: Gaming communities avg. 10-15% monthly communication rate. Nova's 14-18% range exceeds industry standard, with peak 18.4% in December during holiday scrims season.

Vanity URL: 25 (45%)

Discovery: 5 (9%)
Other: 2 (4%)
Insight: 87% of joins via branded channels (vanity URL + member referrals) demonstrates strong brand recognition and organic word-of-mouth growth in competitive CODM community.
Retention Performance

Stable Member Base

Net Growth: +30
Implied Churn: 25 (45%)
Retention Rate: ~55%
Context: Gaming communities typically see 40-60% churn in first 30 days. Nova's 55% retention over 56 days indicates above-average stickiness, supported by automated onboarding and structured scrims.

Outcome

Established Nova Gaming Network as a Tier 1 scrims community with consistently high participation in competitive matches. The brand-first acquisition strategy (45% via custom vanity URL) and above-industry engagement (14-18% communication rate) created a stable foundation for long-term growth. Minimal churn (~1% daily) and 37 server followers demonstrate strong community health and cross-server reputation in the CODM BR ecosystem.
