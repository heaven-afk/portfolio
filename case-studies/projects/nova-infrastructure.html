Project Case Study

Nova Gaming Network

Building a Tier 1 competitive CODM BR scrims community from scratch — complete infrastructure, automated systems, and disaster recovery

Founder June 2024 - Present From Scratch Build
Join Nova Server

Context & Challenge

On June 11, 2024, I founded Nova Gaming Network with the goal of creating a premium competitive Call of Duty Mobile Battle Royale (CODM BR) scrimmage community. The challenge was to build a complete Discord ecosystem from the ground up that could:

  • Support high-frequency competitive scrims with proper structure
  • Provide seamless onboarding for players across multiple regions
  • Scale to handle 1,000+ active members
  • Ensure operational continuity through disaster recovery systems
  • Establish Nova as a Tier 1 scrims destination — the top tier for competitive practice

Key Achievement: Nova Gaming Network successfully reached 1,257 members with consistently high scrims engagement and participation, establishing itself as a Tier 1 scrims community in the CODM BR competitive scene.

Infrastructure Implementation

Operational Evidence: Automated Member Segmentation

Discord's native onboarding feature was configured to implement preference-based member routing—critical for delivering geographically appropriate scrims notifications:

Multi-option preference selection (Event Interests: Scrims, Content, AF Podcast, Memes; Demographics: Gender; Regional Routing) enables automated role provisioning for targeted scrims alerts and community engagement channels.

Segmentation Logic:

  • Event interest routing: Scrims, Content, AF Podcast, Memes, Community News — ensures members only receive notifications for opted-in activities
  • Inclusive demographics: Gender selection supports community-building initiatives and targeted engagement strategies
  • Geographic segmentation: 6-region selection (Africa, Asia, Europe, North America, South America, Australia) enables latency-appropriate scrims matching

6-region selection interface enables members to self-select geographic zones, automatically assigning region-specific roles for scrims notifications tailored to appropriate time zones and latency constraints.

Final onboarding step: opt-in for Nova announcements and event updates—reducing notification noise while maintaining engagement pathways for interested members.

Post-Onboarding Activation:

Members completing the flow are immediately presented with a structured activation checklist designed to reduce passive membership and drive initial engagement:

Step-by-step "Get Started" checklist (Know About Us, Read Server Rules, Say Hello) provides clear activation pathway, reducing member passivity and establishing behavioral norms for new community entrants.

This automated segmentation and activation system eliminated manual role assignment, reduced onboarding time from ~5 minutes to under 60 seconds, and ensured geographically appropriate scrims routing—directly supporting the 18.4% communication rate achieved through targeted, low-noise notifications.

Structural Evidence: Functional Channel Segmentation

The channel architecture was designed to separate internal operations, public information, competitive infrastructure, and community engagement—enabling permission-based access control and reducing cross-category noise:

Tiered category structure (STAFFs, HOME, NOVA RECORDS, COMMUNITY, SCRIMS) implements role-based visibility and functional separation to streamline workflows, reduce member overwhelm, and enable specialized operational channels.

Category Breakdown:

STAFFs — Internal operations workspace

  • boards — Strategic planning and announcements
  • staff_chat, staff_workspace — Day-to-day coordination
  • staff_template, staff_task — Workflow standardization
  • streamer_chat, staff_results — Content and analytics
  • staff_meeting, event_meeting — Voice coordination

HOME — Public-facing information hub

  • rules — Community guidelines
  • welcome — First touchpoint for new members
  • faq — Self-service support
  • announcements — Official updates
  • community_update — Regular status reports
  • podcast, livestream — Content distribution
  • transfers — Team roster changes

SCRIMS — Competitive infrastructure (primary focus)

  • scrims_alerts — Match notifications
  • scrims_result — Match outcomes and leaderboards
  • scrims_faqs — Ruleset and format documentation
  • lobby_rules — Match conduct guidelines
  • scrims_host — Host coordination
  • scrims_teamslot — Team registration
  • scrims_lobbyresults — Detailed match data

Active scrims results channel demonstrates public match outcome transparency—posting competitive data in real-time to establish accountability, enable performance tracking, and support data-driven improvement for participating teams.

This channel architecture directly supported Nova's foundation-building phase by creating clear operational workflows for staff, transparent competitive infrastructure for scrims participants, and low-noise community engagement spaces—collectively enabling the 18.4% communication rate through targeted, relevant notifications.

COMMUNITY — Engagement and retention

  • server_update — Infrastructure changes
  • lounge — General discussion
  • memes — Community culture
  • media — Content sharing
  • ask_questions, create_topics — Two-way communication
  • quotes — Community highlights

NOVA RECORDS — Performance tracking and archival

Structural Evidence: Permission-Based Role Hierarchy

The 20+ role structure was engineered to enable granular channel permissions across leadership, staff operations, and community tiers:

Hierarchical role structure (Founder → Directors → Staff → Specialized positions: GFX Artist, Data Analyst, Host, Caster) establishes clear authority chains and enables role-specific channel access for operational efficiency.

Role Categories:

Leadership & Administration

  • ● Founder — Top-level authority
  • ● Director — Strategic leadership
  • ● Server Admin — Infrastructure management
  • ● Executives — Department heads
  • ● Administration — Operational oversight

Staff Operations

  • ● Sapphire — Premium staff tier
  • ● Server Mods — Community moderation
  • ● Head Staff — Team leadership
  • ● Scrims Host — Match coordination
  • ● Nova Support — Member assistance

Community & Specialized Roles

  • ● Nova Bots — Automation systems
  • ● VIPs, Partners — Community contributors
  • ● Nova Staffs — General staff members
  • ● Nova Moderator — Content moderation
  • ● Streamer — Content creators
  • ● Data Analyst — Performance analytics
  • ● Podcast Anchor — AF Podcast hosts
  • ● GFX Artist — Visual content creation

Design Rationale: The role structure supports both operational needs (moderation, scrims hosting) and community engagement (content creation, analytics). Color-coding provides immediate visual hierarchy for members.

4. Bot Ecosystem & Automation

Deployed approximately 15 bots across the server to automate critical operations:

Core Bot Stack:

  • Sapphire — Primary utility bot for server management
  • Dyno — Moderation automation and auto-moderation
  • Koya — Community engagement and leveling
  • Custom Bot (Primary) — Proprietary automation tailored to Nova's needs
    • Note: Currently developing an advanced multi-bot system as a separate project
  • Custom Backup Bot — Disaster recovery system
    • Automated backups of channel configurations
    • Role structure preservation
    • Rapid restoration capabilities

Automation Highlights:

  • Onboarding Flow: Discord native onboarding + custom role assignment via bots
  • Scrims Management: Automated match alerts, team registration, and result posting
  • Moderation: Auto-mod rules for spam, inappropriate content, and raid protection
  • Disaster Recovery: Custom backup bot ensures operational continuity in case of malicious activity or accidental deletions
