Major Gaming League / Server Insights

Server Insights

Discord analytics snapshot used to support operational improvements and decision-making.

Timeframe: Sep 24, 2025 – Jan 14, 2026
Disclaimer: Metrics are based on Discord’s native insights exports (aggregated reporting windows).

Executive Summary

  • Membership Growth: Grew from 2,379 to 2,995 (+616, +25.9%).
  • Acquisition Source: Dominated by vanity invites (~80%–96% across monthly reporting snapshots).
  • Engagement Spike: Significant activity increase around Nov 14, 2025 across both text and voice channels.
  • Churn Profile: Early tenure churn (< 1 month) represented ~11%–40% of leavers depending on the period.
Growth Health

Membership Trajectory

  • Start: 2,379 (Sep 24, 2025)
  • End: 2,995 (Jan 14, 2026)
  • Net Change: +616 (+25.9%)
  • Peak: 3,008 (Jan 2, 2026)
Insight: Consistent 25% growth over 4 months confirms the effectiveness of the server restructure. The largest single-day jump (+105) occurred on Oct 15.
Acquisition

Source Attribution

  • Vanity Invite Share (Nov 14): ~96.3% (284/295)
  • Vanity Invite Share (Jan 13): ~89.0% (113/127)
  • Main Invite Link (Jan 21): 17 joins
  • Secondary Invite (Jan 21): 1 join
Insight: Reliance on vanity URL suggests strong brand recall, but highlights a risk if the custom URL is lost. Marketing efforts should diversify entry points.
Engagement

Activation & Retention

  • % Opened Channels (Avg): ~35-40%
  • % Communicated (Avg): ~3-8%
  • Peak Messages (Nov): 10,536 (vs ~4k avg)
  • Speaking Minutes (Nov): 1,523 (vs ~800 avg)
Insight: High channel open rate (40%) but lower communication rate (8%) suggests onboarding friction. Users are looking but not chatting immediately.
Retention

Churn Analysis

  • Retention Rate (Peak): 42.71%
  • Early Leavers (<1 mo): ~30-40% of all leavers
  • Critical Period: First 30 days
Insight: A significant portion of churn happens in the first month. Improving the "Day 1" experience is the highest leverage lever for retention.
